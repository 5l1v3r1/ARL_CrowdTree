Verb chains, modals, and auxiliary verbs

Verb chains contain one or more auxiliary verbs and a non-auxiliary verb. Some examples of auxiliary verbs include will, could, must, be, have, and do.
The cookies can be eaten.

The auxiliary verbs can and be and the non-auxiliary verb eaten are attached sequentially in the tree with the first one on top.

When attaching other things to verb chains (e.g., subjects, object, prepositions), please try to attach to the closest verb. If more than one verb is closest, please attach to the one that makes the most sense. If you can't make up your mind about which one makes the most sense, please attach to the one on the left. In the sentence below, to describes where the letters were being flown (to the North Pole).

The letters were being flown to the North Pole.
