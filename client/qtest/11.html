Questions, relative clauses, and words like who, what, where ...

Many questions are rooted by WH words like who, what, where, and how. In general, please treat the WH word as the root. (Note: Sometimes there may be a few more words at the beginning with the WH word as in By what road should we go? or whose car should we take?. Those cases would have By and car as their respective heads. Don't worry about this too much. It's not too common in the data you'll be annotating.

Why did the chicken cross the road?

Relative clauses often start with a word like who, which, when, where, or that. In the sentence below, who heads a clause that modifies man. Where heads a clause that modifies place. (Note: For people with linguistics training, this may seem a bit strange--please try top follow this convention anyways.)

The man who bought the ice cream dropped it by the place where he lives.
