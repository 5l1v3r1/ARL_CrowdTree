Prepositions and subordinating conjunctions

Prepositions and subordinating conjunctions establish relationships between different parts of the sentence. For example, they can describe when or where something is or occurred. Some examples of these kinds of words are in, on, up, down, before, after, and if.

There are two verbs in the sentence below, went and ate. Went does not depend on anything, so it is the root of the tree.

After we ate lunch, we went to the park by the river.
