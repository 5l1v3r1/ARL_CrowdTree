Adverbs and negation

There are many different adverb words (e.g., slowly, quietly, away, always, never). They can modify many other kinds of words, including verbs, adjectives, and even other adverbs. For the most part, these are probably fairly easy to handle.

One of the more difficult cases may be when there is a chain of verbs like have been eaten. In these cases, please just attach the adverb to the closest verb in the verb chain. If more than one of the verbs is closest, just attach it to the one that makes the most sense to you. If both closest options make just as much sense, please attach it to the one on the left.

I quickly turned around and saw it sneaking quietly away.
Very few people have seen a brightly glowing llama but I just have.

One common adverb is not. Sometimes not is contracted into n't.

A return trip isn't guaranteed.
