Basics

For typical sentences, the root of a tree should be a verb. Verbs are words like is, have, jumps, wrote, etc.

John is tall.

The phrases/clauses/arguments that get connected by the verb should be put under the verb. Here, John and tall are connected by the verb is, so they attach below.

Verbs can have multiple arguments. In the example below, the verb gave has three arguments: goalie, ball, and him.

Determiners, quantifiers, and possessives attach to the nouns they modify. Examples of these include the, a, my, your, all, and both. See in the example below how the words The and the attach under goalie and ball

The goalie gave him the ball.

Adjectives, names, numbers, and compound nouns also attach to the nouns they depend on. Below is an example with a few adjectives.

The joyous old man sniffed the fresh winter air.

The general idea is that things that modify other things should be put under the things they modify.
